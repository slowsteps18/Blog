I think I need to document my current net worth. I don't love laying this information out in public as it feels rather private. In the spirit of trying to be accountable and do things differently this year in I need get over it and to lay down where I am at, so that I can track my progression.

People like to track their networth is different ways; I've seen blown out examples where every item of value under the sun is counted and I've seen where they're ultra lean, including the big ticket items and that is about it. I'm on the side of keeping it as simple as possible, so ultra lean for me. Not to mention I don't think including items like cars or bicycles add a lot of value to understanding my current position, even if they are rather valuable.

Where I'm at right now

Without further adieu...

PPOR (equity) $180,000
Superannuation $180,000
Cash $25,000
Shares $23,000
Crypto $1500

Brings me to a grand total of $409,500.

Not going to lie, now I've seen this number I feel a little better about myself and my position. But I need to remember that neither of the biggest items have much to do with me doing anything particularly well.

My PPOR years was purchased with the intent of living in indefinetly. While I assumed it's value was likely to increase, I certainly don't feel I earned that money (equity) as it wasn't bought as an investment.

The above average superannuation stems from the fact I'm not afraid of working hard. I've actually been working since I was 13 years old (cash) and officially employed and earning super/paying tax since I was 15. Thats 20 solid years of contributing to it. Still, I don't credit this to myself as I didn't put that money away, the government forced it.

This post is not really about analysing it so I'll leave it a that, but I immediately see areas to focus on.
