10 simple steps to a achieve your New Year's Resolutions. Not really.

It's a funny thing, there's usually nothing new about the resolutions themselves, they're the same goals year in year out with the hope of doing better this time. That's me in a nutshell.

I can be driven and committed but when it comes to focusing on something personal (Ã  la new years resolutions) I am a straight up failure.

 

What am I doing wrong?

This isn't meant to be a self-help style blog but it is meant to be about being honest and accountable for my efforts and shortcomings. So to that, here are two things I do know:

  1. I know exactly what my problem is, discipline. I blame no-one and nothing but myself.
  2. I know so many people set resolutions, yet so few plan how to achieve these goals.

I read Atomic Habits by James Clear a couple of years ago and found it fascinating. I particularly enjoyed the author's methods and the concept of starting small and stacking habits together. The distinction between goals and the systems to achieve them is something I can absolutely rally behind.

 

What are my resolutions

I don't want to set any, I feel doomed to repeat myself and only get discouraged.

A small measurable start is a smart move though. With that, the first thing I want to achieve this year is to read Atomic Habits again, with the aim to develop systems to learn how to consistently achieve regular, smaller goals. To begin the habit of achieving.

I guess the below is a list of resolutions, but its intentionally lofty. It's more things I'd like to incorporate into my life more opposed to a set of explicit goals. It's nothing I've spent a great deal of time on, but something I'll hopefully reflect on and define soon.

  • Listen more, talk less.
  • Drink more water. Take water to work and take water to bed.
  • Spent more time doing things I love. Perhaps less time lost on YouTube.
  • Less late nights. Bed by midnight?
  • Continiously declutter.
  • Don't take things personally.
  • Get my consistent lunchtime naps back.
  • Ride my bike more. Consider joining a local ride group?
  • Stretch. For the love of god I need to stretch more.
  • Use more to do lists, stop trying to remember everything.
  • Less sugar.
